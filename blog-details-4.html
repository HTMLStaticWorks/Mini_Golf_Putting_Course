Choosing Your Putter: Blade vs. Mallet

June 05, 2026 By Coach Sterling 3 Min Read

Choosing the correct putter shape is the single most important gear decision a golfer can make. The debate between a sleek blade putter and a high-stability mallet putter has gone on for generations.

While blade putters offer traditional aesthetics and precise feedback control, mallet putters rely on modern engineering to prevent twisting on off-center impacts. Here is how to understand which design suits your putting stroke.

1. The Classic Blade Putter

Blade putters have a traditional, clean rectangular design. They tend to be toe-balanced, meaning they are built for putters who have a natural arc in their stroke. If your putting stroke swings inside-to-square-to-inside, a blade putter provides the natural gate release you need.

"Your putter is an extension of your stroke. If you have a natural arc, choose a blade; if your stroke is straight-back and straight-through, go with a mallet." — Coach Sterling

2. The Modern Mallet Putter

Mallet putters feature a larger, deeper head profile. They offer high MOI (Moment of Inertia), which means the clubhead resists twisting on off-center hits. They are typically face-balanced and work best for players with a straight-back, straight-through stroke.

3. Choosing What Fits You

Test both putters on our practice green. A blade putter offers better distance control feel, whereas a mallet putter provides high alignment confidence. Choose the one that fills you with confidence standing over the ball.
