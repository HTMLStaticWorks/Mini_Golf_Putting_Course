Ultimate Kid's Party Planning Guide

June 10, 2026 By Party Crew 4 Min Read

Hosting a children's birthday party can feel like coordinating a major public event. At PuttMasters, we host hundreds of parties a year, and we've gathered our best secrets to keep kids engaged and parents stress-free.

From choosing custom timelines to determining the exact food portions, proper preparation is the difference between a smooth event and complete chaos. Here is our expert checklist for putting together the ultimate kid's outing.

1. Create a Strict but Fun Timeline

Do not let the kids run wild immediately upon arrival. Start with 45 minutes of scheduled putting on the course, followed by 30 minutes in the private party room for pizza and cupcakes, and end with 15 minutes of free play or arcade games. A structured schedule keeps kids focused and prevents fatigue.

"The secret to a successful kid's party is a balance of structured competition and free play. Give them a goal on the course, then let them celebrate." — Party Crew Coordinators

2. The Cupcake Calculation Formula

Always plan for 1.5 cupcakes per child. Kids love the frosting but often leave half of the cake behind. Cupcakes are much easier to distribute than a traditional custom cake, require no slicing tools, and minimize cleanup time.

3. Fun Putting Challenges

Add excitement by playing 'Bingo, Bango, Bongo' style rules: give small prizes for the first ball on the green, the closest to the cup, and the lowest overall score. This keeps all children active, even those who aren't natural putters.
